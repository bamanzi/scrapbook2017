                                                          Kubuntu is buggy for the time being.

                                                          Last stable Kubuntu version is Kubuntu 14.04 and 14.10. They switched to KDE5 on 15.04 and did not have enough time to make it stable in 16.04. As intermediate versions have constant kernel updates and KDE5 is still in active development, the intermediate Kubuntu versions are still very much developer versions.

                                                          KDE Neon solved this by using Ubuntu 16.04 LTS as a stable base. Since 5.7 it has been stable. I use KDE Neon User LTS edition (the most stable one) and it is indeed rock stable. I changed every setting and installed all my software and only had a few minor issues that disappeared after reboot. In fact in combination with the Nouveau drivers it is even more stable then Ubuntu Unity. In Unity I nouveau drivers have always given me problems. But I use nVidia drivers anyway for gaming and those never let me down.

                                                          As KDE Plasma development is nicely going along you can expect Kubuntu 18.04 to be stable again, but I would recommend in between versions as main desktop.

                                                          KDE5 is moving lightning fast. It has only been around two years and already stable. Compare that with Gnome3 that is only becoming stable after SIX years. Gnome3 is even OLDER than Unity7. Unitu7 was made stable within two years as well (12.04).
